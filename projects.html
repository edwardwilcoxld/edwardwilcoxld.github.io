Agatha Christie Thrillers

These two plays were an exciting contrast – one set in a sterile hospital, the other in a seaside resort. Each demanded unique lighting moods: cold blues and clinical whites for 'The Patient'; warm ambers and textured gobos for 'An Afternoon at the Seaside'. 'The Patient' especially had to be precisely rehearsed for many of the lighting cues.

❮❯

Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat

This musical was all about colour, rhythm and energy. I programmed colour chases and animated rainbow transitions to mirror the energy of each song, while maintaining key focus for solos. Particular songs were a nice contrast, for example the deep blues and greens for the sadder, more thoughtful songs such as 'Close Every Door'. The finale was a full-stage spectacle synced perfectly to the music.

The Addams Family

A wonderfully eccentric show that let me play with colour eerie purples and greens for the Addams home, and bright pastels for the “normal” world of the Beinekes. It was particulary fun to play with a subtle haze to create that spooky, theatrical glow the audience loved.

Where is Mrs Christie

The lighting for this particular play was quite tastefully simple, a one-woman show only required a simple 3 states: the outside evening, a cosy interior, and a state with a sharp focus on the radio.
