Agatha Christie Thrillers

These two plays were an exciting contrast – one set in a sterile hospital, the other in a seaside retreat. Each demanded unique lighting moods: cold blues and clinical whites for one; warm ambers and textured gobos for the other. I used precise cue timing to heighten suspense in every twist.

Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat

This musical was all about colour, rhythm and energy. I programmed layered colour chases and animated rainbow transitions to mirror the energy of each song, while maintaining key focus for solos. The finale was a full-stage spectacle synced perfectly to the music.

The Addams Family

A wonderfully eccentric show that let me play with contrast – eerie purples and greens for the Addams home, and bright pastels for the “normal” world. I used dynamic fades, chase sequences, and subtle haze to create that spooky, theatrical glow the audience loved.

Where is Mrs Christie

A gripping mystery that brought the classic Agatha Christie suspense to life on stage. Lighting was used to emphasise tension, highlight clues, and create a dynamic mood shift between scenes.
